The African traditional religion or African religion are simply the religious beliefs of the people of Africa. Africa is a vast continent encompassing both geographical variation and several cultural diversity. African religious practices generally manifest themselves in communal ceremonies or divinitory rites. Every ethnic group in Africa has developed a complex and distinctive set of religious beliefs and practices. One of the major features of African traditional religion are festivals. In African traditional religion, festivals are very important factors and it is very critical aspect of the African society. They are not only important for the observation of time which can be seen in reality through the season of the year, but it can also serve the purpose of mobilization and cohesion. Every aspect of the traditional African societies marked with one festival out another.1Festivals in African traditional religion are in form of rituals, sacrifices and ceremonies. There are countless number of festivals celebrated in traditional African societies, they include: Eyo festival in Nigeria, mombas a carnival in Kenya and so on. Amongst the Yoruba people of Nigeria, festivals serve as a connector of the social world of the Yoruba with the unseen world. The yoruba Pantheon consists of 100s of Gods worshipped for an immense variety of purpose, each representative of some natural or spiritual element or human emotions. Other gods are natural objects in thier environment such as mountains, hills and rivers that have influenced people's lives and cultural history.2
